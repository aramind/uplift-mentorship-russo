How Air Quality Sensors Work

Air quality sensors are compact devices designed to detect and measure various pollutants present in the air. These pollutants include gases, particulate matter, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that can affect human health and the environment. The sensors convert raw measurements into understandable metrics like the Air Quality Index (AQI), which helps you easily interpret whether the air is clean, moderate, or hazardous.

Depending on their design, sensors use advanced technologies such as laser scattering, infrared absorption, or electrochemical sensing to identify specific pollutants in real time. Many modern sensors combine multiple detection techniques to provide a more complete and accurate snapshot of air quality.

Once the sensor collects pollutant data, it processes the readings through built-in microcontrollers and transmits them to the cloud via Wi-Fi, cellular, or LoRa connections.
