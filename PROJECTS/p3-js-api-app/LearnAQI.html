The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized scale that measures how clean or polluted the air is in a particular location. It translates complex scientific data from air sensors into a simple number between 0 and 500. The lower the number, the cleaner the air ‚Äî the higher the number, the greater the level of pollution and potential health risks.

These pollutants can come from vehicles, factories, wildfires, and even household activities.

Governments and organizations around the world use AQI data to inform people when air pollution levels become unsafe. A high AQI can trigger public health alerts, school advisories, or encourage citizens to stay indoors. Understanding AQI helps us make smarter, safer decisions for our families and communities.
